# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a thing that people do on the internet where they try to trick people into giving away important information like passwords or bank details. They do this by pretending to be someone you trust.

Sometimes a student will get an email or message that looks like it is from a bank or a website they use. The message will say something like you have to do something now or your account will be closed. It will have a link that takes you to a website that looks a lot, like the one.

If you put your login information or bank details into this website the bad people will get them. They might even ask for a code that your bank sends to your phone so they can take your money.

Students are often the ones who get tricked by phishing because they use the internet a lot and they might not always check if a link is real. If you get phished you could lose money someone could steal your identity. They could get into your personal accounts.

Phishing is a deal and it shows how important it is to be careful when you are online and to know what to look out for. Phishing is something that you should always be aware of when you're using the internet.
